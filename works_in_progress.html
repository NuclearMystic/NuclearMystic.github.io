Night Shift Nightmare

Night Shift Nightmare is the biggest and most advanced game I have worked on so far, and it's also the first game I've colaborated with other designers on. Night Shift Nightmare is a first person horror game, with story elements that change the outcome of what happens to the player based on their actions. The game is about a lone night security guard at a shady warehouse on the outskirts of town who has a habit of falling asleep on the job between patrols. Eventually strange things start to happen until you find yourself face to face with a horrifying creature.

My roles in the project:

  • -Lead programmer. All code in game is made from scratch by me.
  • -Level design. While I did not make the models themselves, I am responsible for setting them up in the scenes.
  • -Lighting and occlusion. The game is designed to take advantage of culling objects that are not directly in the players view.
  • -Cutscenes, dialogue, and events. While I did not write a bulk of the lines, and events in the game, I did setup and direct them, and create all the triggers for how they should proceed.

Important Features:

  • -First person camera with a full functioning 3rd person body that bends and moves with players controls.
  • -Equipment system allowing character to toggle a taser, and a flashlight on and off.
  • -Advanced monster AI. It will walk around the level searching for the player, and run after and attack after spotting them.
  • -Patrol system with animated guidelines directing the player where to go.
  • -Interacteable security camera system allowing the player to move cameras and see other parts of the level.
  • -Fully functional options menu with options for Audio levels seperated for Music, SFX, and Ambience, FOV, resolution, graphic settings, Toggling between fullscreen and windowed, and mouse sensitivity.
